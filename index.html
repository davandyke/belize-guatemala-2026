NoteSunday Recap

Early Uber to the airport and a smooth series of flights—managed to get some solid napping in along the way. Upon landing at Flores (FRS), I discovered the hotel hadn’t actually arranged the shuttle as they’d claimed, so I hired a private taxi for the trek to Tikal. Once checked into the Jungle Lodge, I got the sunrise tour booked for tomorrow. It kicks off at 4:00 AM local time, which feels a little less hostile when you realize it’s 6:00 AM back in Michigan. Showered, grabbed some dinner, and now it’s time to get to bed on time.

Jungle Lodge

  • Originally built in 1956 as a base camp for University of Pennsylvania archaeologists excavating Tikal
  • Rustic cabins with jungle views, restaurant, pool, and bar
  • Located just outside the park entrance, surrounded by jungle and wildlife

Monday, March 16 — Tikal National Park → Flores

  • 4:00–8:00 AM — Sunrise Tour
    • Buy park tickets before entering — no tickets sold inside the park, sunrise tickets must be purchased in advance
    • Up to 80% chance of cloud cover in the morning — mist is likely but the experience is still worth it
    • Bring bug spray, water, snacks, and sun protection
  • 8:00–9:00 AM — Breakfast at the Jungle Lodge
  • 9:00 AM–1:00 PM — Main Tour of Tikal
    • Key sights:
      • Templo IV — one of the few temples you can climb; commanding views across the jungle canopy
      • Gran Plaza — Templo I & II facing each other; clap your hands in the center to hear a quetzal-like echo; climb Templo II for photos
      • El Mundo Perdido — astronomical observatory with Teotihuacán-style architecture
      • Acrópolis del Norte — funerary complex, richly detailed
      • Birders: ramón trees in the Gran Plaza attract aracari toucans and slaty-tailed trogons
  • ~1:00 PM — Return to lodge, grab stored luggage
  • 2:00 PM — Shuttle from Jungle Lodge to Flores island (~1.5 hrs)
  • 3:30 PM — Check into Los Amigos Hostel (Pay Q600 cash)
    • Flores → San Ignacio shuttle and San Ignacio → Caye Caulker shuttle + water taxi booked at Los Amigos
  • Dinner (~7:30 PM):
    • Maracuyá: The top recommendation. Rickety dock entrance, butterfly sanctuary, and incredible lakefront dining. Creative Guatemalan menu. (Closed Tuesdays).
    • Terrazzo (Alternative): Relaxed rooftop Italian with great lake views if you’re not in the mood for Maracuyá.
  • Nightcap:
    • Secret Garden (Los Amigos): Right at your hostel. Famous for the best margaritas on the island and a reliable social hub that stays active until 1:00 AM.
NoteMonday Recap

3:30 AM wakeup for the 4:00 AM sunrise tour. Walked through the ruins with flashlights in the dark — a small group with a knowledgeable local guide — and climbed to the top of Templo IV, the highest temple at Tikal, about 200 feet up. Howler monkeys making a ruckus in the canopy below — they sound exactly like Chewbacca from Star Wars, which is fitting since the Rebel base scenes on Yavin 4 in the original Star Wars were actually filmed right here at Tikal. The mist was too thick to see any actual sunrise, but sitting above the jungle treetops in the early morning fog was something I won’t forget. Toucans and parrots started flying between the temples as the sky brightened — saw several parrots up close, and despite their colorful markings they’re mostly green and blend in surprisingly well with the leaves. After “sunrise,” walked through a section of Tikal on the way back to the restaurant for breakfast — eggs, beans, fresh fruit, and fresh-squeezed mixed fruit juice. Packed up, checked out, and headed back into the ruins for the main tour. Impressive structures rising straight out of the jungle, some over a thousand years old — hard to imagine how they were designed and constructed, and easy to lose yourself imagining Maya life in those days. The Gran Plaza was the highlight, with two massive temples facing each other across a grassy courtyard. Back to the Jungle Lodge for lunch, then the shuttle to Flores, a colorful little town on an island on the shores of Lago Petén Itzá. Took a shower, relaxed in a hammock outside my room, then walked the full loop around the island to catch the sunset over the lake. Dinner at Maracuyá, a lakeside restaurant where I had pepián chicken — a traditional Guatemalan stew that was excellent. They even had a friendly restaurant dog who came to visit and laid at my feet for a few minutes. Early bedtime despite a nap on the shuttle from Tikal — the 3:30 AM wakeup caught up with me.

Tuesday, March 17 — Flores → San Ignacio, Belize

  • 7:00 AM — Shuttle from Flores/Santa Elena toward the Belize border (~3-4 hrs including border crossing). Booked at Los Amigos.
  • Cross at Benque Viejo / Melchor de Mencos — Expect a wait
  • ~10:00–11:00 AM — Arrive San Ignacio. Drop bags at Bella’s Backpackers (check-in may not be until afternoon).
    • 4 Galves St, San Ignacio | Private room with private bathroom
  • Late breakfast / early lunch in San Ignacio:
    • Pop’s Restaurant — Belizean breakfast institution, fry-jacks, flour tortillas, open 6am–3pm
    • Martha’s Kitchen — local Belizean breakfast, hash fish, longaniza eggs, 6am–3pm
    • Running W (San Ignacio Resort Hotel) — international menu, birding at breakfast — aracaris perch nearby, 7am–9pm
  • San Ignacio Market (5am–5pm, best on Saturdays) — Maya, Mennonite, Creole and Mestizo vendors; local produce, crafts, cheap street food. Worth a wander even midweek.
  • Afternoon: Take it easy or pick one activity:
    • Cahal Pech ruins (6am–6pm) — 5-min drive or uphill walk, dates to 1200 BCE, one of oldest Maya settlements in Belize. Self-guided 1.5 hrs. Great birding (motmots, trogons). Sunset views from the A1 temple over town.
    • Xunantunich ruins — 30 min away, massive site with El Castillo pyramid and incredible views, hand-cranked river ferry
    • Tubing on the Macal or Mopan River — lazy jungle float, great way to meet people

Wednesday, March 18 — ATM Cave (Actun Tunichil Muknal)

Mayawalk Tours

  • Meet at Mayawalk office, 19 Burns Avenue, San Ignacio at 7:30 AM (between Tandoor Restaurant and Belize Bank)
  • 45-min drive to Tapir Mountain Nature Reserve, 45-min jungle hike to cave entrance
  • ~3 hours inside — swimming, wading, climbing through chambers to the Crystal Maiden
  • Lunch included, back in San Ignacio by ~4:30 PM
  • Balance due on day: $73.77
Tip

What to wear/bring:

  • Hiking shoes/trainers with closed toes (will get wet) + flip flops to change into
  • Socks (mandatory)
  • Shorts and shirt that can get wet and muddy
  • Extra dry clothes for after
  • Small backpack, reusable water bottle, bug spray, snacks
  • Printed waiver (front and back on one sheet) + valid ID
  • No cameras or electronics — government ban.

Local Reef — Friday, March 20 · 8:15 AM – 12:30 PM

  • Dive 1 & 2: Two local reef sites
  • Surface interval: Stingray Village — fruits and snacks
  • Marine life: Turtles, moray eels, colorful coral canyons, reef fish

Esmeralda — Saturday, March 21 · 8:15 AM – 1:30 PM

  • Dive 1: Tackle Box
  • Surface interval: San Pedro (~45 min–1 hr) — grab lunch or explore
  • Dive 2: Esmeralda — the shark dive!
  • Marine life: Caribbean reef sharks, nurse sharks, grouper, snapper, lobster, occasional hammerhead

Blue Hole — Monday, March 23 · 5:30 AM – 4:00 PM

  • Dive 1: Great Blue Hole — stalactites, stalagmites, reef sharks, eagle rays
    • Advanced Open Water: 130 ft / 40 m
  • Dive 2: Half Moon Caye Wall
  • Surface interval: Half Moon Caye — lunch + explore island, Red-footed Boobies
  • Dive 3: The Aquarium, Long Caye Wall — turtles, marine life

Other activities:

  • The Split — The island’s social hub; Lazy Lizard Bar & Grill (pool, bar, diving platform), Sip N Dip overwater bar. Full Moon Parties monthly.
  • Tarpon Feeding Dock — Free. Calle La Posa, opposite the street BBQs. Dozens of massive tarpons (up to 8ft) swim below the dock. Remarkable and free.
  • Stingray beach at Iguana Reef Inn — Daily 4pm to sunset, dozens of stingrays arrive in the shallows. Seahorse reserve nearby. Skip the feeding, but worth watching.
  • BBQs near the Split — Jerk chicken, shrimp kebabs, whole fish, grilled lobster. Betty’s, Ester’s, Chef Kareem’s. Great prices, fire up from lunchtime.
  • Snorkeling: Hol Chan Marine Reserve & Shark Ray Alley (most popular); Caye Caulker Marine Reserve (coral gardens, the Swash, Shark Ray Village). Operators: Caveman Snorkeling Tours, Nauti Time Tours, Stressless Eco Friendly Tours.
  • Manatee tour at Swallow Caye Wildlife Sanctuary — 19 miles away, guided boat tour, sightings not guaranteed but often rewarded. Ask any operator.
  • Kayaking through the mangroves — Calm west side of island. Good Vibes Tours rents clear-bottomed kayaks.
  • Eating: Amor y Café (breakfast, 6:15am–12:15pm), Errolyn’s House of Fry Jacks (local breakfast staple), Maggie’s Sunset Kitchen (Belizean seafood, sunset dock, 11:30am–2pm & 5–9pm), Meldy’s Restaurant (local Belizean), Il Pellicano Cucina Italiana (garden Italian, pizzas and pastas, 5–9:30pm), Pasta per Caso (Italian, reserve ahead via WhatsApp 602-6670, Wed–Sun dinner)
  • Drinking: The Pelican Sunset Bar (sunset drinks), I&I Reggae Bar (three levels, hammocks, panoramic views, 4pm–2am Mon–Sat), Taproom (only brewery on the island, beer flights, 10:30am–11pm)
